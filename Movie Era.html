Year 2002

Spider Man

Key Details

Release Date: 3 May 2002

Director: Sam Raimi

Writer: David Koepp

Distributor: Columbia Pictures (Sony Pictures)

Box Office: Made over $821 Million worldwide, breaking records at the time.

Cinematic First: The first film in Hollywood history to cross $100 Million in a single weekend.

Major Comic Deviations: This version featured organic web-shooters from Peter's wrists rather than mechanical web-shooters constructed by Peter.


Main Cast

Tobey Maguire as Peter Parker / Spider-Man

Willem Dafoe as Norman Osborn / Green Goblin

Kirsten Dunst as Mary Jane Watson

James Franco as Harry Osborn

J.K. Simmons as J. Jonah Jameson

Rosemary Harris as Aunt May

Cliff Robertson as Uncle Ben


Story Summary

The Origin:

Nerdy teen Peter Parker is bitten by a genetically engineered "Super-Spider". He gains Incredible strength, wall-crwaling abilities, and organic wrist web-shooters

The Villian:

Meanwhile, industrialist Norman Osborn tests an unstable military serum on himself. It drives him insane, turning him into the high-tech, flying Green Goblin.

The Tragedy:

Out of spite, Peter lets a thief escape. That same thief later murders his Uncle Ben. Devastated by guilt, Peter adopts his uncle's motto "With great power comes great responsibility" and becomes Spider-Man.

The Climax:

Norman learns Peter's identity and kidnaps Peter's crush, MJ. Spider-Man saves her and defeats the Goblin in a brutal final battle.

The Ending:

Norman is accidentally killed by his own flying glider, His son, Harry blames Spide-Man and vows revenge. To keep MJ safe from future enemies, Peter hides his feelings and chooses a lonely life as a hero.

Year 2004

Spider-Man 2

Key Details

Release Date: 30 June 2004

Director: Sam Raimi

Screen Writer: Alvin Sargent

Distributor: Columbia Pictures (Sony Pictures)

Box Office: Grossed over $788 Million worldwide

Major Accolade: Won the Academy Award for Best Visual Effects

Main Cast

Tobey Maguire as Peter Parker / Spider-Man

Alfred Molina as Dr. Octto Octavius / Doctor Octopus (Doc Ock)

Kirsten Dunst as Mary Jane Watson (MJ)

James Franco as Harry Osborn

Rosemary Harris as Aunt May

J.K. Simmons as J. Jonah Jameson

Story Summary

The Struggle:

Two years later, Peter's personal life is a disaster. He is broke, failing college, losing his love Mary-Jane to an engagement, and his best friend Harry wants Spider-Man dead.

Powers Lost:

The intense stress manifests physically, causing Peter to lose his superpowers. Exhausted, he throws his suit in the trash and decides to live a normal life

The New Threat:

Meanwhile, a brilliant nuclear scientist named Dr. Octto Octavius conducts a failed fusion experiment. His wife is killed, and four intelligent mechanical tentacles become permanently fused to his spine, warping his mind into the villainous Doctor Octopus.

The Return:

Doc Ock needs money and resources to rebuild his dangerous machine. When Doc Ock kidnaps MJ, Peter realizes he cannot run from his destiny. His powers return, and he flights Ock in an iconic train battle.

The Climax:

Peter unmasks himself to stop Ock's unstable reactor. Moved by Peter's sacrifice, Octavius regains control of his mind and drowns himself along with his machine to save New York.

The Resolution:

During the chaos, Mary Jane and Harry both discover Peter is Spider-Man. MJ leaves her wedding to be with Peter, while a betrayed Harry discovers his father's secret stash of Goblin gear, setting up a dark path.

Peter's life is finally going well, and he plans to propose to MJ. However, things fall apart when Harry Osborn seeks revenge as the New Goblin, and escapee Flint Marko transforms into Sandman after an accident. Peter soon lears that Sandman was the acutual killar of Uncle Ben.

Year 2012

The Amazing Spider-Man

Key Details

Release Dte: 3 July 2012

Director: Marc Webb

Distributor: Columbia Pictures (Sony Pictures)

Box Office: Grossed over $757 Million Worldwide

Major Change: This Reboot reintroduced Peter Parker's comic accurate mechanical web shooters and shifted the love interest from MJ to Gwen Stacy......

Main Cast

Andrew Garfield as Peter Parker / Spider-Man

Emma Stone as Gwen Stacy

Rhys Ifans as Dr.Curt Connors / The Lizard

Denis Leary as Captain George Stacy

Martin Sheen as Uncle Ben

Sally Field as Aunt May

Story Summary

The Secret Past:

Peter Parker is an estranged, tech-savvy high schooler who discovers a hidden briefcase belonging to his late father, Richard Parker. This leads him to Oscorp and his father's former partner, the one armed scientist Dr. Curt Connors

The Bite and Tragedy:

While sneaking around oscorp, Peter is bitten by a genetically modified Spider, granting him superhuman abilities. Later, following an argument with Uncle Ben, Peter allows a convenience store thief to escape. That same thief kills Uncle Ben moments later, driving Peter to become a masked vigilante to hunt the killer.

The Lizard Rises:

Peter hels Dr.Connors complete a cross-species genetic formula to regrow human limbs. Connors tests the serum on himself it successfully regrows his arm but drives him insane, transforming him into a giant, violent creature called The Lizard

The Conflict:

Dr. Connors plans to release a chemical gas across New York City to turn the entire population into lizard-hybrids. Meanwhile, Peter falls in love with his classmate Gwen Stacy, whose father happens to be NYPD Captain George Stacy, the man hunting Spider-Man.

The Ending:

Spider-Man and Captain Stacy team up at the top of Oscorp tower to stop the gas release. They cure Dr.Connors, but Captain Stacy is mortally wounded by the Lizard. Before dying, he makes Peter promise to stay away from Gwen to keep her safe. Peter skips Captain Stacy's funeral but subtly hints to Gwen that he intends to break that promise.

The Ghost of the Past:

The Spark of Electro:

Max Dillon, a lonely Oscorp electrical engineer, falls into a tank of genetically modified electric eels. Instead of dying, he turns into a living generator of electricity called Electro. After a misunderstanding in Times Square, Electro feels betrayed by Spider-Man and vows to destroy him.

Peter's childhood friend, Harry Osborn, returns to run Oscorp but learns he is dying from a hereditary illness. Believing Spier-Man's blood can cure him, Harry asks Peter for help. Electro and the Green Goblin team up to shut down New York's power grid. Spider-Man' defeats Electro with Gwen's he,p, but the Goblin suddenly attacks and realizes Peter is Spider-Man. To hurt Peter, Goblin drops Gwen from the top of a clock tower. Peter catches her with a web-line, but sudden whiplash snaps her neck, killing her instantly.

The Hopeful Return:

Devastated by Gwen's death, Peter quits being Spider-Man for five months. However, after listening to an old graduation speech given by Gwen about hope, Peter finds the strength to put the suit back on and returns to protect the city against a new villian, Rhino.

Year 2016

Captain America: Civil War

Key Details

Release Date: 6 May 2016

Directors: Anthony and Joe Russo

Distributor: Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures

Box Office: Grossed over $1.15 Billion Worldwide

MCU Debut: This film officialy Spider-Man into the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU).

Nain Cast

Tom Holland as Peter Parker / Spider-Man

Robert Downey Jr. as Tony Stark / Iron Man

Chris Evans as Steve Rogers / Captain America

Marsia Tomei as Aunt May

Short Summary

The Recruitment:

The Avengers are Deeply divided over the Sokovia Acoords (a law to regulate superheroes). When Captain America goes rogue to protect his friend, Iron Man (Tony Stark) needs backup. Tony tracks down a 15-year-old Peter Parker in Queens, New York, after discovering viral videos of the boy's superhero antics.

The Upgrade:

Tony visits Peter's apartment and talks to him about his motives. Impressed by Peter's sense of responsibility, Tony recruits him for the mission. In exchange, Stark replaces Peter's homemade hoodie-suit with a high-tech, advanced Spider-Man suit featuring Mechanical eyes and web-shooters.

The Airport Clash:

Spider-Man is flown to Germany and makes a dramatic entrance at the Leipzig-Halle Airport by stealing Captain America's shield. A massive battle erupts between Team Iron Man & Team Cap.

The Battle:

Despite his nervousness and constant fanboy talking, Peter holds his own against seasoned heroes. He uses his web-shooters to flight Falcon and Winter Soldier, and famously uses a strategy inspired by the Empire Strikes Back to bring down a giant-sized Ant-Man.

The Aftermath:

During the battle, Peter is knocked down and bruised. Tony Stark, worried for the teenager's safety, tells him he is done for the day and orders him to go home. In the post-credits scene, Peter is back in Queens playing with his new Stark-tech web-shooters, which project a hidden "Spider-Signal" onto his ceiling, teasing his future adventures.

Spier-Man Homecoming

Distributor: Sony Pictures / Columbia Pictures

Box Office: Grossed over $880 Million worldwide

MCU Connection: This movie skipped the traditional spider-bite origin story entirely, focusing instead on Peter trying to prove himself to Iron Man.

Main Cast

Tom Holland as Peter Parker / Spider-Man

Michael Keaton as Adrian Toomes / Vulture

Robert Downey Jr. as Tony Stark / Iron Man

Zendaya as Michelle "MJ" Jones

Jacob Batalon as Ned Leeds

Laura Harrier as Liz Allan

Story Summary

The High-Tech Mentorship:

Following the events of Civil War, 15 year old Peter Parker returns to high school while waiting his next big mission from Tony Stark. His Best friend Ned accidentlly learns Peter is Spider-Man. Frustrated by handling small local crimes, Peter desperately wants to becaome an official Avenger.

The Scvanger Villian:

Meanwhile, Adrian Toomes runs a criminal enterprise selling advanced weapons built from salvaged alien technology left behing from past Avengers battles. He glies using a mechanical wingsuit, operating under the alias Vulture.

The Tough Love:

Peter disobeys Stark's orders and tracks down Toomes gang. A botched confrontation on a staten Island ferry splits the cruise ship in half, forcing Iron Man to step in and save everyone. Deeming Peter too reckless and reliant on tech, Stark confiscates the high-tech Spider-Man suit, leaving Peter devastated.

The Shocking Twist:

Trying to live a normal life, Peter asks his crush, Liz to the homecoming dance. When he arrives at her house, he is shocked to discover that Liz's father is actually Adrian Toomes (Vulture). During the drive to the dance, Toomes deduces Peter's secret identity and threatens to kill him if he interferes again.

The True Hero:

Peter ditches the dance anyway. Wearing his original homemade hoodie-suit, he confronts Vulture, who is trying to hijack a Stark cargo plane filled with Avengers Weapons. After a brutal fight, the plane crashes on a beach. Peter saves Toomes life from his own exploding wingpack and leaves him webbed up for the police.

The Choice:

Impressed by Peter's bravery, Tony Stark offers him a spot on the Avengers and show him a new "Iron Spider" suit. Peter matures and declines, choosing to remain a neighborhood hero for now. The film ends with Aunt May accidentally walking into Peter's room while he is in costume, discovering his secret.

Year 2018

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse

Key Details

Release Date: 14 December 2018

Directors: Bob Persichetti, Peter Ramsey, and Rodney Rothman

Distributor: Sony Pictures Releasing

Box Office: Grossed over #384 Million worldwide

Major Accolade: Won the Academy Award (Oscar) for Best Animated Feature Film

Style Note: This movie pioneered a revolutionary animation style that blended 3D computer graphics with traditional hand-drawn comic book techniques

Main Cast (Voice Actors)

Shameik Moore as Miles Morales / Spider-Man

Jake Johnson as Peter B. Parker / Spider-Man

Hailee Steinfeld as Gwen Stacy / Spider-Woman

Mahershala Ali as Aaron Davis / Prowler

Brian Tyree Henry as Jefferson Davis

Liev Schreiber as Wilson Fisk / Kingpin

Nicolas Cage as Spider-Man Noir

Story Summary

The New Spider:

Miles Morales is an Afro-Latino teenager struggling to fit into an elite Brooklyn boarding school. While spray-painting graffiti in an abandoned subway station with his cool uncle Aaron, Miles is bitten by a radioactive spider and quickly develops weird new powers including camouflage invisibility and bio-electric venom blasts

The Multi-Verse Opens:

Mob boss Wilson Fisk (Kingpin) builds a massive particle accelerator to open parallel dimensions, hoping to bring back alternate versions of his deceased wife and son. The original universe's Peter Parker tries to stop him but is brutally murdered by Kingpin. Before dying, Peter gives Miles a flash drive to destroy the accelerator

The Teachers and Variants:

Grieving and lost, Miles meets an older, out-of-shape, and depressed Peter B. Parker from an alternate universe, who was sucked into Miles' world. Soon, four other alternate spider-heroes arrive through the rift: Gwen Stacy (Spider-Woman), Spider-Man Noir, Penington Parker (SP//dr), and Spider-Ham

The Tragic Betral:

The alternate heroes don't think Miles is ready to fight. Things get worse when Miles discovers that his beloved uncle Aaron is actually the Prowler, a ruthless assassin working for Kingpin. During a rooftop chase, Aaron refuses to kill his nephew when he recognizes him, leading Kingpin to shoot and kill Aaron

The Leap of Faith:

Tied up by the other Spider-heroes for his own safety, Miles breaks free, masters his powers, and takes a literal "leap of faith" off a skyscraper to suit up in his own black-and-red costume. He joins the other heroes at Kingpin's collider for the final battle

The Ending:

Miles uses his unique venom-blast ability to defeat Kingpin and helps all the alternate Spider-heroes return safely to their own dimensions. Miles fully embraces his new identity as Brooklyn's one and only Spider-Man. In a post-credits scene, Miguel O'Hara (Spider-Man 2099) travels through time, setting up the multi-verse sequel.

Year 2019

Spider-Man: Far From Home

Key Details:

Release Date: 2 July 2019

Director: Jon Watts

Box Office: Grossed over $1.13 Billion Worldwide

MCY Timeline: This movie serves as the official epilouge to the MCU's Phase 3, dealing heavily with the emotional aftermath of Tony Stark's sacrifice

Main Cast:

Tom Holland as Peter Parker / Spider-Man

Jake Gyllenhaal as Quentin Beck / Mysterio

Zendaya as Michelle "MJ" Jones

Samuel L. Jackson as Nick Fury

Jon Favreau as Happy Hogan

Jacob Batalon as Ned Leeds

Marisa Tomei as Aunt May

Story Summary

The Grief and the Trip

Eight months after the events of Endgame, Peter Parker is mourning the loss of his mentor, Tony Stark. To escape the pressure of being the next "Iron Man", Peter decides to go on a shool sciene trip to Europe, planning to finally confess his feelings to his classmate MJ

The New Hero

The trip gets hijacked when Nick Fury recruits Peter to fight giant elemental monsters attacking European cities. Peter teams up with Quentin Beck, a heroic warrior claiming to be from an alternate dimension destroyed by these creatures. The media names him Mysterio

The Trust and Betrayal

Believing Beck is the hero the world needs, Peter hands over "EDITH" a high-tech glasses system left to him by Tony Stark that controls a massive orbital satellite drone network. This turns out to be a fatal mistake; Beck is actually a disgruntled former Stark Industries employee using holographic illusions and stealth drones to fake the monsters

The Final Illusion

MJ deduces that Peter is Spider-Man and helps him find a piece of dropped drone tech that exposes Mysterio's fraud. Peter travels to London, where Mysterio plans a massive fake "Avengers-level" attack. Using his pure instincts ("Spider-Sense"), Peter cuts through the illusions, defeats Beck, and regains control of EDITH

The Cliffhanger Ending

Mysterio is seemingly killed by a stray drone shot during the battle. Back in New York, Peter and MJ start a relationship. However, in a shocking mid credits scene, controversial news host J. Jonah Jameson broadcasts a leaked, doctored video created by Beck. The video frames Spider-Man for the London drone attack and publicly exposes Peter Parker's real identity to the entire world.

Year 2021

Spider-Man No Way Home

Key Details

Release Date: 17 December 2021

Director: Jon Watts

Distributor: Sony Pictures / Columbia Pictures

Box Office: Grossed a massive $1.92 Billion worldwide, becoming one of the highest-grossing films of all time.

The Ultimate Crossover: Brought back iconic villains and heroes from Sam Raimi’s and Marc Webb’s past Spider-Man franchises into the main MCU timeline.

Main Cast

Tom Holland as Peter Parker / Spider-Man

Tobey Maguire as Peter Parker / Spider-Man

Andrew Garfield as Peter Parker / Spider-Man

Zendaya as MJ

Benedict Cumberbatch as Dr. Stephen Strange

Willem Dafoe as Norman Osborn / Green Goblin

Alfred Molina as Otto Octavius / Doctor Octopus

Jamie Foxx as Max Dillon / Electro

Jacob Batalon as Ned Leeds

Marisa Tomei as Aunt May

Story Summary

The Broken Spell:

With his secret identity exposed by Mysterio, Peter Parker’s life becomes a nightmare. He, MJ, and Ned are rejected from colleges due to the controversy. Desperate, Peter asks Doctor Strange to cast a spell making the world forget he is Spider-Man. Peter tampers with the spell while it is being cast, causing it to fail and accidentally rip open the Multiverse

The Multi-Verse Villains:

Villains from alternate universes who knew Peter Parker's identity are dragged into the MCU, including Doc Ock, Green Goblin, Electro, Sandman, and Lizard. Instead of sending them back to their universes to die, Peter traps Doctor Strange in the Mirror Dimension so he can try to "cure" the villains of their corrupt powers first

The Ultimate Tragedy:

The experiment goes horribly wrong when the Green Goblin's dark persona takes over Norman Osborn. A brutal fight breaks out, and the Goblin kills Aunt May with his glider. Before dying, she delivers the iconic MCU version of the line: "With great power, there must also come great responsibility"

Three Spider-Man Unite:

Heartbroken and broken-spirited, Peter goes into hiding. Ned and MJ use Strange's sling ring to look for him, but instead, they accidentally summon two alternate versions of Peter Parker: the "Peter-Two" (Tobey Maguire) and "Peter-Three" (Andrew Garfield). The older Spiders find Tom Holland's Peter and comfort him, sharing their own experiences with grief and loss

The Battle of Liberty Island:

The three Spider-Men team up at the Statue of Liberty, working like a brotherhood to successfully cure all the villains. In a deeply emotional moment, Andrew Garfield's Spider-Man saves MJ from falling, finding redemption for his failure to save Gwen Stacy. Tobey Maguire's Spider-Man stops Tom Holland from brutally killing the Green Goblin in a fit of rage

The Ultimate Sacrifice:

As the Multiverse begins to completely fracture and tear the sky open, Doctor Strange returns. Tom Holland's Peter realizes the only way to save reality is to cast a new spell: making everyone forget who Peter Parker is entirely. He says a tearful goodbye to MJ and Ned, promising to find them. The film ends with Peter completely alone in the world, living in a cheap apartment, wearing a homemade comic-accurate suit, and continuing his lonely duty as a hero.

Year 2023

Spider-Man Across the Spider-Verse

Key Details

Release Date: 2 June 2023

Directors: Joaquim Dos Santos, Kemp Powers, and Justin K. Thompson

Distributor: Sony Pictures Releasing

Box Office: Grossed over $690 Million worldwide

The Cliffhanger: Unlike the previous movies, this film is the first part of a two-part story, ending on a massive, unresolved cliffhanger

Main Cast (Voice Actors)

Shameik Moore as Miles Morales / Spider-Man

Hailee Steinfeld as Gwen Stacy / Spider-Woman

Oscar Isaac as Miguel O'Hara / Spider-Man 2099

Jason Schwartzman as Jonathan Ohnn / The Spot

Jake Johnson as Peter B. Parker / Spider-Man

Issa Rae as Jessica Drew / Spider-Woman

Daniel Kaluuya as Hobart "Hobie" Brown / Spider-Punk

Story Summary

The Growing Distance:

More than a year after Kingpin's defeat, Miles Morales is struggling to balance high school with being Brooklyn's full-time Spider-Man, all while missing Gwen Stacy. Meanwhile, Gwen joins an elite, multi-dimensional team of Spider-heroes called the Spider-Society, led by the serious and intense Miguel O'Hara (Spider-Man 2099)

The New Threat:

Miles encounters a clumsy new villain named The Spot, whose body is covered in interdimensional portals. The Spot blames Miles for his mutation, which happened when Kingpin's collider exploded. As the movie progresses, The Spot learns to travel across dimensions, gathering power to destroy everything Miles loves

The Spider-Society:

Gwen secretly visits Miles, but Miles follows her through a portal into the Multiverse. He arrives at the Spider-Society headquarters on Nueva York, meeting hundreds of Spider-variants. There, Miguel O'Hara explains the concept of "Canon Events"—tragic milestones that must happen to every Spider-hero (like the death of a police captain close to them) to keep their universe from collapsing

The Rebel Choice:

Miles realizes his own father, who is about to be promoted to Police Captain, is destined to be killed by The Spot to fulfill the canon event. Refusing to let his father die, Miles rebels against the Spider-Society. Miguel tries to imprison him, leading to a massive, chaotic chase where Miles outsmarts hundreds of Spider-heroes

The Shocking Twist:

Miles escapes using a dimensional teleportation machine that tracks a user's DNA. However, because the radioactive spider that bit Miles originally came from an alternate dimension (Earth-42), the machine mistakenly sends him to Earth-42 instead of his home world

The Cliffhanger Ending:

On Earth-42, Miles finds a dark world where his father is already dead, there is no Spider-Man, and his alternate self has actually become the villainous criminal known as The Prowler. Back in the main universe, The Spot arrives to kill Miles' father. The film cuts to black just as Gwen forms a rogue team of old friends (including Peter B. Parker, Spider-Punk, and Spider-Ham) to track down and rescue Miles.
