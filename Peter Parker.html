Early Life

The Beginning

Peter Benjamin Parker was born in Queens, New York. His parents, Richard Parker and Mary Parker, worked as secret agents before they mysteriously disappeared when Peter was very young.

After losing his parents, Peter was raised by his loving Aunt May and Uncle Ben in Forest Hills, Queens.

Although Peter grew up without his parents, May and Ben gave him a happy childhood and taught him kindness, honesty, and responsibility.

School Life

Midtown High School

Peter Parker was a brilliant science student at Midtown High School in Queens, New York. Although he was extremely intelligent, he was socially awkward and often became the target of bullying.

His biggest bully was Flash Thompson, while Harry Osborn eventually became one of his closest friends. Peter spent most of his free time studying science and working on experiments.

During his school years, Peter also met Gwen Stacy and later Mary Jane Watson, who became two of the most important people in his life.

The Spider Bite

The Day Everything Changed

During a science exhibition, Peter Parker was bitten by a genetically engineered (or radioactive, depending on the version) spider. At first, nothing seemed unusual, but within hours his body began changing in extraordinary ways.

Peter developed incredible strength, enhanced agility, the ability to climb walls, and a mysterious danger warning known as the Spider-Sense.

This single accident transformed an ordinary teenager into Spider-Man.

The Death of Uncle Ben

The Greatest Lesson

After gaining his new powers, Peter initially used them for fame and money instead of helping others. One day, he allowed a criminal to escape because he believed it wasn't his responsibility.

That same criminal later murdered Uncle Ben during a robbery. Peter was devastated and blamed himself for the tragedy.

Uncle Ben's death completely changed Peter's life. From that day forward, he dedicated himself to protecting innocent people as Spider-Man.
